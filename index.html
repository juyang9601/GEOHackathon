About the Shakaim Project

The goal of the Shakaim Project is to recognize the contribution of the Indigenous Shuar People to climate change mitigation, emphasizing the benefits of reforestation, and enhancing the transfer of Indigenous knowledge between the Elders and youth. There are 4 components to the project:

1) Scientific component: It weaves together Indigenous knowledge of climate change with scientific data (e.g. changes in rainfall and temperature) before and after reforestation. Biomass estimates based on satellite observations and drone data are used to quantify the amount of carbon stored by newly planted trees. Continuous observations over the reforested areas allow us to assess the resilience of the new forests.

2) Outreach with the youth: in special workshops, the Shaur youth learn from their Elders how their Indigenous knowledge has been used to conserve nature and their biocultural heritage, but also how to disseminate their own stories, create interactive maps, and learn how to interpret satellite and drone data of their area.

3) Radio Program on Medicinal Plants: Shuar Elders will disseminate their knowledge of medicinal plants to the youth through a dedicated radio program.

4) Reforestation of the Amazon: The Shakaim project will continue the work started byÂ  the NUMI project that concluded in August 2021 and involved several different Indigenous communities including women, youth and Elders, working together on reforesting degraded lands in the Amazon with native plants such as fruit trees, medicinal plants and timber. It involved the reforestation of 400 hectares across three different countries - Ecuador, Peru and Bolivia.
